Cover Letter

I consider myself a “Math Guy”, so when it came to English classes in high school, it was always a struggle or just boring to me. Going into ENC 1101 I was expecting a very similar experience, however right out of the gate my assumption was proven wrong. Very quickly what we learned flipped writing classes on their head, I learned that writing was descriptive not prescriptive, that it is shaped by not only the person writing, but what it was that they were writing, and who they were writing for. This website you are reading this on can be considered a portfolio of some of my assignments, including a video on literacy narrative, and essay analysis on literacy practice and even a remediation project, as well as a refection of each of them. Below is a short overview of each of the our Student Learning Objectives(SLOs) that drove our course.

Writing Processes & Adaptations
If you didn’t get this from the name of this SLO, writing is a process, there isn’t a single formula for how to write something good, sadly. As stated before when you are writing there are many different factors that manipulate what is being written and how it is being written. In my remediation project I needed to convert on of my previous assignments into some new genre, off the bat forcing us students to build something that was different. We not only had to come up with a new type of literacy but also try and adapt our previous piece into this new genre without losing the meaning of the piece.

Multiple Literacies & Goal Setting
This course required students to not only write, we learned that literacy is not soully alphabetic text. We learned to except and lean into multimodality in our literature, whether that is video, audio, pictures, text, it helps to bring the reader in and allows them to better follow what you are conveying. In my assignment about literacy narrative, we had to use multimodality I was originally going to write an essay using alphabetic text, but later I was informed that the information might be better in a video format sense the way I write has a lot of jokes and sarcasm that might be lost in writing.

Variation across Contexts
This SLO was sort of the bread and butter of the course. No one writes in the same way, even as I have said the same person will write different ways for different reasons. In this course we went farther, we brought up code meshing which is bringing in your cultural background into your writing, really the goal of this SLO is to allow us to understand that there are different ways of writing and to embrace that difference.

Decision Making & Production
This goes without saying but for every project we had we needed to make some sort of decision. What genre are you working with, who is the audience, what is the limitations of the genre, what modes you can use in this genre. The remediation project was filled with choices, which may have resulted in unseen difficulties. I chose to turn my first assignment into a video game, as you can see I like to involve my major into my work. The video game genre is very different from a video, mainly because you aren’t telling the reader the story, the reader is experiencing the story. In my opinion this can engage your reader at least more than a book, however I had to make choices on what to remove from the video to make the game more “game like”. The production part of this SLO is mostly the acting part of making a decision and acting on it.

Writing and Power
This course allowed me to find the power behind my words, at least a bit. When I am writing a essay on The Great Gatsby I’m not very invested on the symbolism of Gatsbys car showing the coolness and calmness of the character turning into panic. However, when I am writing an essay about a group of friends I am in that I play tabletop games with like I did for my analysis of a literacy group assignment, it is more personal and in turn the way I write is stronger. Writing and power go hand in hand and being able to put your heart behind your words has always been important.

Revision
In this class the revision process was not only highlighted, accented. I always knew that getting feedback on any product you are working on is important but this class magnified that. Every major assignment we had at least one peer review and we would also get feedback from the professor himself. My peer who reviewed my first major assignment was the one who recommended me to turn my essay into a video, looking back on it, it might have been a bit late to completely change genres, but the feedback was key to the final product and made it more interesting in the end.
